Urban Heat Island Analysis Module

The Urban Heat Island (UHI) module is an environmental analysis feature integrated into the Smart GECI platform.

Urban Heat Island refers to the phenomenon where urban areas experience higher temperatures than surrounding rural regions.

This effect occurs due to factors such as dense construction materials, reduced vegetation, and human activity.

Purpose of the Module

The UHI module allows users to analyze temperature patterns and environmental conditions using satellite-derived raster datasets.

Key Capabilities

Raster Layer Visualization

Thermal raster datasets representing land surface temperature can be visualized on the interactive map.

Pixel-Level Analysis

Users can click on specific locations on the map to retrieve temperature values associated with that pixel.

Area of Interest Analysis

Users can define a spatial boundary representing an area of interest. The system then calculates statistical temperature values for that area.

Heat Classification Mapping

Temperature values are categorized into different heat intensity levels to identify heat-prone zones.

Decision Support

The analysis results help planners and researchers understand environmental heat patterns and support sustainable planning decisions.
